- **Ahmad Yousif AlFaraj**, an independent consultant operating from Jubail Industrial City, Saudi Arabia, holding national ID/Iqama no. **{{ahmad_id_number}}** *(or: Commercial Registration no. {{cr_number}})* (the "**Consultant**"); and
- **{{client_legal_name}}**, a {{client_entity_type}} organized under the laws of {{client_jurisdiction}}, with its registered office at {{client_address}} (the "**Client**").

The Consultant and the Client are each referred to as a "**Party**" and collectively as the "**Parties**".

## 1. Definitions

- **"Services"** means the software development, consulting, and related professional services to be performed by the Consultant for the Client as described in one or more SOWs.
- **"SOW"** or **"Statement of Work"** means a written statement of work executed by the Parties under this Agreement, in substantially the form attached as Schedule A.
- **"Deliverables"** means the tangible and intangible items the Consultant agrees to deliver under an SOW.
- **"Confidential Information"** has the meaning given in Section 8.
- **"Fees"** means the amounts payable by the Client to the Consultant as set out in an SOW.

## 2. Engagement

2.1 The Client engages the Consultant to provide the Services on the terms of this Agreement.

2.2 The Services are non-exclusive. The Consultant may provide similar services to other clients, provided this does not breach Section 8 (Confidentiality) or any specific exclusivity expressly agreed in an SOW.

## 3. Statements of Work

3.1 Each engagement under this Agreement is governed by a separate SOW. Each SOW shall, at minimum, identify: (a) the scope; (b) deliverables; (c) timeline and milestones; (d) fees and payment schedule; and (e) acceptance criteria.

3.2 If there is a conflict between this Agreement and an SOW, this Agreement prevails unless the SOW expressly states that a specific clause overrides this Agreement.

3.3 Any change to an SOW must be agreed in writing (including email) by both Parties as a "**Change Order**".

## 4. Fees, Invoicing, and Taxes

4.1 **Fees.** The Client shall pay the Fees set out in each SOW. Unless an SOW states otherwise, all amounts are quoted in **Saudi Riyals (SAR)**, exclusive of VAT.

4.2 **VAT.** The Consultant will add VAT at the prevailing rate (currently 15%) to all invoices where applicable, and will issue tax invoices compliant with ZATCA e-invoicing requirements.

4.3 **Invoicing.** Unless the SOW provides otherwise, the Consultant will issue invoices: (a) on signature of the SOW for any deposit; (b) on completion of each milestone; and (c) on final acceptance.

4.4 **Payment terms.** Invoices are payable within **fourteen (14) calendar days** of issue, by bank transfer to the account identified on the invoice.

4.5 **Late payment.** If an undisputed invoice remains unpaid after the due date, the Consultant may: (a) charge an administrative service fee of **SAR 250** per invoice plus **SAR 50 per calendar day** of continued non-payment, as a contractual fee (not interest), to cover the cost of follow-up and administration; and (b) suspend Services after giving five (5) business days' written notice. *(`[REVIEW REQUIRED]` — confirm Sharia-compliance of late-fee wording.)*

4.6 **Disputed invoices.** If the Client disputes any portion of an invoice, the Client shall notify the Consultant within seven (7) days of receipt, pay the undisputed portion on time, and the Parties shall resolve the disputed portion in good faith within fifteen (15) days.

4.7 **Expenses.** Pre-approved expenses (e.g., travel, third-party software licences purchased on the Client's behalf) will be billed at cost plus an administrative handling fee of five percent (5%).

## 5. Acceptance

5.1 Each Deliverable is deemed accepted on the earlier of: (a) the Client's written acceptance; or (b) the lapse of **five (5) business days** from delivery without the Client raising a written objection identifying specific non-conformance with the acceptance criteria.

5.2 If the Client raises specific non-conformance, the Consultant will remedy the non-conforming items at no additional charge within a reasonable period and resubmit for acceptance.

## 6. Intellectual Property

6.1 **Pre-existing IP.** Each Party retains ownership of all intellectual property it owned before, or develops independently of, this Agreement ("**Pre-existing IP**").

6.2 **Work product.** Subject to Section 6.3, on full payment of all Fees due in respect of a Deliverable, the Consultant assigns to the Client all right, title, and interest in the Deliverable (the "**Work Product**").

6.3 **Reserved rights.** The Consultant retains ownership of: (a) any tools, libraries, frameworks, methodologies, and reusable code components that are not Client-specific ("**Consultant Tools**"); and (b) general know-how, skills, and experience gained while performing the Services. The Consultant grants the Client a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive licence to use Consultant Tools to the extent embedded in the Work Product.

6.4 **Portfolio rights.** The Consultant may identify the Client and describe the Services performed in the Consultant's portfolio, marketing materials, and case studies, provided no Confidential Information is disclosed and the Client has not requested otherwise in writing.

## 7. Independent Contractor

7.1 The Consultant is engaged as an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, agency, partnership, joint venture, or fiduciary relationship between the Parties.

7.2 The Consultant is responsible for the Consultant's own taxes, social insurance contributions, and any business registration obligations.

## 8. Confidentiality

8.1 **"Confidential Information"** means any non-public information disclosed by one Party ("**Discloser**") to the other ("**Recipient**") that is marked confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential, including business plans, source code, customer data, financial information, and unreleased product information.

8.2 The Recipient shall: (a) use Confidential Information only to perform under this Agreement; (b) protect it with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care; and (c) not disclose it to any third party except to employees, contractors, or advisers with a need to know who are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

8.3 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes public other than through the Recipient's breach; (b) the Recipient already lawfully knew without confidentiality obligations; (c) is independently developed by the Recipient without use of Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality obligations.

8.4 **Duration.** This Section survives termination of this Agreement for **three (3) years**, except that trade secrets are protected for as long as they remain trade secrets.

## 9. Data Protection

9.1 If the Services involve processing Personal Data (as defined under the Saudi Personal Data Protection Law and its implementing regulations, "**PDPL**"), the Parties will agree a separate Data Processing Addendum.

9.2 The Consultant will: (a) process Personal Data only on the Client's documented instructions; (b) implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures; (c) notify the Client without undue delay of any actual or suspected personal data breach; and (d) on termination, return or delete all Personal Data as instructed.

## 10. Warranties

10.1 Each Party warrants that: (a) it has authority to enter into this Agreement; and (b) entering into this Agreement does not breach any other obligation it owes to a third party.

10.2 The Consultant warrants that the Services will be performed with the care, skill, and diligence reasonably expected of a competent professional in the field.

10.3 **Disclaimer.** Except as expressly stated in this Agreement, the Services and Deliverables are provided "as is" and the Consultant disclaims all other warranties, express or implied, including merchantability and fitness for a particular purpose, to the maximum extent permitted by law.

## 11. Limitation of Liability

11.1 Neither Party is liable to the other for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, or for lost profits, lost revenue, lost data, or business interruption, even if advised of the possibility of such damages.

11.2 Each Party's total aggregate liability arising out of or in connection with this Agreement (whether in contract, tort, or otherwise) is capped at the **total Fees paid by the Client to the Consultant in the six (6) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim**.

11.3 The caps in Sections 11.1 and 11.2 do not apply to: (a) breach of confidentiality; (b) infringement of the other Party's intellectual property; (c) the Client's obligation to pay undisputed Fees; or (d) liability that cannot be limited under applicable law.

## 12. Indemnification

12.1 The Consultant will defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the Client against any third-party claim that the Work Product (excluding Client-supplied materials and Consultant Tools licensed openly) infringes the third party's intellectual property rights, provided the Client promptly notifies the Consultant and reasonably cooperates with the defence.

12.2 The Client will defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the Consultant against any third-party claim arising from Client-supplied materials or instructions, except where the Consultant has actual knowledge that following such instructions would infringe a third party's rights.

## 13. Term and Termination

13.1 This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated under this Section.

13.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience on **thirty (30) days'** written notice, provided no SOWs are active. If any SOWs are active at the date of notice, this Agreement continues until those SOWs are completed or separately terminated.

13.3 Either Party may terminate this Agreement or any SOW immediately on written notice if the other Party: (a) commits a material breach not cured within fifteen (15) days of notice; (b) becomes insolvent, enters bankruptcy, or has a receiver appointed; or (c) breaches Section 8 (Confidentiality).

13.4 **Effects of termination.** On termination: (a) the Client will pay for all Services performed and expenses incurred up to the termination date; (b) the Consultant will deliver all completed Work Product for which Fees have been paid; and (c) Sections 6, 8, 9, 11, and 16 survive.

## 14. Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for any failure or delay caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, acts of government, war, terrorism, civil unrest, pandemics, or failure of the public internet, provided the affected Party gives prompt notice and uses reasonable efforts to mitigate.

## 15. Notices

Notices must be given in writing to the addresses set out at the beginning of this Agreement, or to such other address as either Party notifies in writing. Notice by email is sufficient where: (a) sent to the contact email identified in the most recent SOW; and (b) acknowledged by reply.

## 16. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

16.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **Kingdom of Saudi Arabia**, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

16.2 The Parties will attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute through senior-level negotiation for fifteen (15) days before initiating formal proceedings.

## 17. General

17.1 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement (together with each SOW) is the entire agreement between the Parties on its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions.

17.2 **Amendments.** No amendment is effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

17.3 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written consent, except to a successor by merger or sale of all or substantially all assets.

17.4 **Severability.** If any provision is held invalid, the remainder continues in effect.

17.5 **Counterparts and electronic signature.** This Agreement may be signed in counterparts and by electronic signature (DocuSign, qualified Saudi e-signature, scanned PDF), each of which is an original.

17.6 **Language.** The English version of this Agreement is the controlling version. *(`[REVIEW REQUIRED]` — for KSA-only engagements, consider executing in Arabic.)*
