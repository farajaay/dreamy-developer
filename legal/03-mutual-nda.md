# Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

> **[REVIEW REQUIRED]** Working draft. Have a Saudi-qualified lawyer review before signing.

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("**Agreement**") is entered into as of **{{effective_date}}** between:

- **Ahmad Yousif AlFaraj**, an independent consultant of {{ahmad_address}} ("**Party A**"); and
- **{{other_party_name}}**, a {{entity_type}} of {{other_party_address}} ("**Party B**").

Each a "**Party**" and together the "**Parties**".

## 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship in relation to **{{purpose_description, e.g., "the design and development of a customer-facing web application"}}** (the "**Purpose**"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose Confidential Information (defined below) to the other.

## 2. Confidential Information

2.1 **"Confidential Information"** means any non-public information disclosed by one Party (the "**Discloser**") to the other (the "**Recipient**"), in any form (written, oral, visual, electronic), that is marked confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature or the circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to:

- business plans, strategies, financial information, customer and supplier lists;
- product roadmaps, source code, technical specifications, architecture diagrams;
- personal data of employees, customers, or end users;
- pricing, fee structures, and commercial terms.

2.2 **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) is or becomes public other than through the Recipient's breach of this Agreement;
(b) was already lawfully in the Recipient's possession without confidentiality obligations before disclosure;
(c) is independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Confidential Information; or
(d) is rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality obligations.

## 3. Use and Protection

3.1 The Recipient shall:

(a) use Confidential Information only for the Purpose;
(b) protect it with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar sensitivity, and in any event with no less than reasonable care;
(c) limit access to its employees, contractors, and professional advisers who: (i) need to know it for the Purpose; and (ii) are bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement; and
(d) not reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble any software or technology disclosed as Confidential Information.

3.2 The Recipient is responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its employees, contractors, or advisers.

## 4. Permitted Disclosures

The Recipient may disclose Confidential Information if required by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority, provided that the Recipient: (a) gives the Discloser prompt written notice (where legally permitted) before disclosure; and (b) cooperates with the Discloser, at the Discloser's expense, in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy.

## 5. No Licence; No Warranty

5.1 Confidential Information remains the property of the Discloser. No licence or other right under any patent, trademark, copyright, or other intellectual property right is granted by this Agreement, except the limited right to use Confidential Information for the Purpose.

5.2 Confidential Information is provided "as is". Neither Party makes any warranty, express or implied, as to its accuracy or completeness.

## 6. Term and Return of Information

6.1 This Agreement takes effect on the date first written above and continues for **two (2) years**, unless earlier terminated by either Party on thirty (30) days' written notice.

6.2 The confidentiality obligations in this Agreement survive termination or expiry of this Agreement for a further **three (3) years**. Trade secrets remain protected for as long as they qualify as trade secrets under applicable law.

6.3 On the Discloser's written request, the Recipient shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential Information of the Discloser in its possession (including all copies, extracts, and summaries), and certify in writing that it has done so. The Recipient may retain one (1) copy in its secure archives solely to comply with legal or record-keeping requirements, subject to ongoing confidentiality.

## 7. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Discloser is entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief, in addition to any other remedies available at law.

## 8. No Obligation to Proceed

Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to enter into any further agreement, transaction, or business relationship. Each Party may terminate discussions at any time without liability.

## 9. General

9.1 **Entire agreement.** This Agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties on its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions.

9.2 **Amendments.** Any amendment must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

9.3 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written consent.

9.4 **Severability.** If any provision is held invalid, the remainder continues in effect.

9.5 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be signed in counterparts and by electronic signature, each of which is an original.

## 10. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **Kingdom of Saudi Arabia**. Any dispute will be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the **Commercial Court of {{ksa_city}}**.
